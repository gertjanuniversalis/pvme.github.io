    Ripper Demons

    ​

    Introduction

    Ripper Demons are one of the 4 Raptor Mobs, and provide by far the highest EXP rates among the group. This guide will go through the methods which AoE ripper demons.

    ​

    ​

    Low kph single target killing for undergeared players will not be elaborated on in this guide. In short, bring your best melee gear with , and and use the ripper demon cave with the midday skybox.

    ​

    Stats

    XP per kill : 2159.5 + 712.6

    XP per kill : 2721.5

    Optimal kills per hour: ~1650 (~930 )

    Optimal XP per hour : ~4.49M (~2.53M )

    ​

    Desirable Drops

    Ripper Demons have a valuable loot table containing: Wines of Zamorak, Rune salvage, coins and a variety of high level herbs.

    This makes them one of the better tasks to do for gp.

    ​

    When killed with Binding Contracts in your inventory, they are captured and turned into a Binding Contract (Ripper Demon)

    This provides a large buff to the GP/h of Ripper Demons, currently adding an extra 74,191 in revenue per kill.

    ​

    For the Slayer log, Ripper Demons also drop:

    Mainhand and offhand Ripper Claws

    Cursed Amascut Sand

    The 5 Miner's journals

    Required for

    The Parasitic orb

    A pet item that unlocks the Nipper Demon pet.

    Dropped at a rate of 1/30,000 from Ripper Demons and 1/333 from Elite Ripper Demons.

    ​

    Location and How to Get There

    The two most popular locations to kill ripper demons are:

    1. The Ripper Demon cave, South-east of the Al-Kharid lodestone through a shortcut near the eastern gate. The ripper demons here are not aggressive.

    2. The Ripper Demon horde west of demonic ruins. The ripper demons here are aggressive.

    a. This is the location used for conventional slaying of rippers, as slayer in the wilderness is now risk-free.

    b. You can get here using the teleport to the Herb patch or the games necklace teleport to Corporeal Beast. Travelling via the demonic ruins is risky as you may aggro a Kal'gerion demon and get spec'd.

    ​

    Mechanics

    Slaughter
    When engaged from a distance, Ripper Demons will immediately teleport to the player and cast Slaughter , which deals triple damage if you move. This makes them extremely easy to stack up for AoE damage. This also incentivises staying at the same spot when AoEing.
    Death from above
    Ripper Demons gain adrenaline whenever they autoattack. When the bar fills up, they will jump and land where the player was standing shortly after, dealing up to 32,000 hard typeless damage resulting in an instant kill. This can be avoided by moving away from your original location immediately upon seeing them jump. When AoEing, good DPM is cruicial to avoid this mechanic.
    Aggression
    If the player is below 35% HP, all nearby Ripper Demons will immediately aggro to the player. Usually, using when low HP is desirable to prevent being stacked out.
    Death spin
    When killed, Ripper Demons will spin and hit any nearby players with weak rapid melee hits per tick in a 3x3 area, which can deal large damage if stood in. This mechanic is the main reason and are essential for killing rippers.

    Method: AFK Magic

    The only method which allows ripper demons to be AFK'd involves the use of an alt along with combat training dummies . The method works by immediately gaining the aggression of any ripper that spawns, causing them to jump into a spot they cannot walk out from, thus preventing them from attacking you.

    ​

    Preset

    Preset Suggestion & Breakdown
    AFK Magic

    Strategy

    By setting up a melee (red box) and alt (yellow box) below, one can trap Ripper Demons in an area once they jump to you with such that they cannot continue attacking you.

    It will be helpful to set an Alt1 alert on the alt account to prevent it from logging out.

    ​

    IMPORTANT NOTE (Courtesy of #Lourens)

    If the alt method is done properly, Rippers should not be able to attack you AT ALL after they jump.

    If you are getting attacked, your alt probably got trampled. When a Ripper or another player passes through your alt, the alt becomes unable to block rippers, thus breaking the triangle.

    Quick explanation of trampling: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qCZKk8pCmjc

    The simplest way to fix this is to move your alt away and then back to the spot.

    ​

    The only lasts for 10 minutes before expiring, so it is best to reset the setup when refreshing by picking up your dummy and cannon and re-entering the cave to reset ripper aggro.
